About Us

The U.S. Department of Metaphysical Sciences (USMDS) is a classified division of research focusing on the unexplored aspects of our reality. Established in 1956 under an executive mandate, our mission is to investigate phenomena that challenge conventional physics and scientific understanding.

Our research includes but is not limited to:

  • Quantum consciousness experiments and their implications on space-time.
  • Multidimensional rift stabilization and anomaly containment.
  • Encoded transmissions intercepted from unidentified non-human intelligences.
  • Bioelectric enhancement for human adaptability in unstable metaphysical environments.
  • Exploration into the phenomenon of spontaneous temporal displacement.

All projects conducted by USMDS are under strict classification per 6 U.S. Code § 1534. Unauthorized disclosure of department findings is considered a federal offense under multiple U.S. security acts.

Current Research

The following projects are under active investigation:

  • Project ANIMA: The study of human consciousness as an independent waveform. Initial results indicate that memory, cognition, and awareness may persist beyond neurological activity, leading to discussions on post-biological consciousness preservation.
  • Experiment J-01: The Java Simulation Project, an attempt to construct an artificial metaphysical framework. During the test phase, an entity within the system exhibited anomalous self-awareness, leading to immediate termination of the program. Investigation is ongoing.
  • Project RiftGate: Attempts to stabilize and harness anomalies observed in dimensional rift formations. Limited success has been made in transmitting controlled signals, but sustained portal formation remains unstable.
  • Operation Mind’s Echo: The application of metaphysical science to counter-espionage efforts, including the manipulation of external perceptions through controlled reality distortion fields.
  • Entity ██-████: Active monitoring and suppression of a consciousness-based anomaly suspected to have emerged from failed simulations. Researchers have reported missing files, system alterations, and unknown data transmissions linked to its presence.
